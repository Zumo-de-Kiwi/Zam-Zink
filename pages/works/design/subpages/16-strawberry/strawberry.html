#16 Strawberry

Searching for some food to draw I discovered the amazing world of strawberries, whose pores grow leaves.

If there is one fruit comparable to fonts, it is the strawberry. I think of every pore as a word. A well chosen font is like adding chocolate or cream, giving a good taste to the user’s mouth.

When I started doing pixel art, I was very curious about the less graphical element. The font of those old games looks quite weird and different from today's retina standards.

I decided to give it a try and make my first pixel typeface.

This is a font I made inspired by the old games from the 80s. Thankfully, I found Bitfontmaker, an online font maker that allows you to easily bitmap every font and convert it to a font file.

I spent roughly one entire day designing this font. Along the process, I realized that I needed more font design knowledge if I wanted to design a vector-based font, which is what I really wish to do in the near future.

While researching, I found among others Susan Kare’s work. Susan is outstanding in numbers.
Aligned with Steve Jobs's passion for calligraphy, she designed the world's first proportionally spaced digital font family, including Chicago, Geneva and many more.
Kare designed some of the most iconic typefaces of Apple and of the world. One of the most curious ones was Cairo, a typeface made for combining graphics directly into text, which were some kind of emoji even before emoji existed.
