How To Use

There is a WHY and WHAT section in every work. Some will have WHERE as well.

why

Where I state why I choose that object to represent my work, and explain the design process behind it.

where

Where I briefly describe either the historical context of this work, where the inspiration comes from or where this topic originated.

what

Where I outline what this project is about, its purpose, my source of inspiration, the process and other details.

What I learned

Some of the reference items helped me discover the beauty of design, i. e. they taught me that something with a valuable design is not only visually attractive but is also endowed with a satisfactory usability; for example, a building entrance door that you can intuitively know whether you need to pull or push, the wheel of an iPod which you know how to handle immediately, an easy to read magazine layout or a website that guides you with its UX patterns throughout its interface.
